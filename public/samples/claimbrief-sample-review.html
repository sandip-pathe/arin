ClaimBrief organizes claim documents for professional review. It does not provide legal advice, public adjusting services, claim negotiation, carrier submission, or coverage determinations.

Claim Overview

Insured Maria Lopez reported a residential wind/hail roof claim at 418 Cedar Ridge Lane, Austin, Texas, under policy HO-783921-TX with Lone Star Mutual Insurance. The date of loss is April 18, 2026, and the claim was reported on April 20, 2026. Lone Star Mutual denied roof replacement in a May 12, 2026 letter, attributing the condition to wear, deterioration, and improper maintenance. 1

Documents Reviewed

Carrier denial letter

May 12, 2026 denial letter citing wear and tear, deterioration, improper maintenance, and no observed storm-created opening. 1

Policy excerpts

Coverage A, duties after loss, exclusions, and suit limitation language from the claim packet. 2

Contractor estimate

May 19, 2026 contractor estimate totaling $18,420 for roof and interior items. 3

Reviewer notes

Notes mention hail marks on soft metals/gutters and missing engineer report or moisture readings. 4

Carrier Denial Or Underpayment Reasons

  • Roof replacement was denied because observed roof conditions were described as long-term wear, deterioration, and improper maintenance. 1
  • Interior ceiling staining was denied because the carrier stated no storm-created opening was observed. 1
  • The carrier estimate was $1,280 for minor repairs below the $2,500 deductible, while the contractor estimate was $18,420. 3

Policy Provisions And Deadlines Found

  • Coverage A applies to direct physical loss to the dwelling unless an exclusion applies. 2
  • Duties after loss include prompt notice, protecting the property from further damage, showing damaged property, and providing requested records and documents. 2
  • Listed exclusions include wear and tear, marring, deterioration, latent defect, faulty workmanship, and neglect. 2
  • Suit against the company must be brought within two years and one day after the date of loss. 2

Missing Evidence Checklist

  • Engineer report addressing storm-created damage versus wear.
  • Moisture readings for the upstairs hallway staining.
  • Photo set mapping hail marks, soft metals, gutters, roof slopes, and interior damage.
  • Written response explaining why the contractor estimate was not addressed in the carrier letter.
  • Any prior roof maintenance records if wear/improper maintenance is disputed.

Draft Response Outline For Human Review

  1. Identify the carrier's stated reasons for denial in its own wording.
  2. Map each stated reason to the relevant policy exclusion or duty language.
  3. Compare the carrier's $1,280 repair estimate with the contractor's $18,420 scope.
  4. Ask the carrier to explain whether the contractor estimate and soft-metal/gutter photos were considered.
  5. List missing expert or moisture evidence for the licensed reviewer to request or obtain.

Questions For The Licensed Reviewer

  • Is there enough evidence to dispute the wear-and-tear conclusion?
  • Does the interior staining need additional proof of storm-created opening?
  • What estimate line items are most important to reconcile first?
  • Are there any approaching policy or statutory deadlines?
